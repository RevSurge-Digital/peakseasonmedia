Contact Peak Season Media — Claim Your Free Marketing Audit

Tell us what you run and what's keeping you up at night — the summer slump, the ad account that eats money, the empty Tuesday. We'll reply with a free marketing audit: real observations about your business, in writing, within one business day.

What the Free Marketing Audit Covers

Not a sales deck — a working document about your actual presence:

  • Your Google footprint: Business Profile health, Maps rankings for the searches that matter on your block, review velocity, and what AI assistants currently say when someone asks about places like yours.
  • Your paid-ads picture: if you're running Google or Meta ads, where the spend is leaking; if you've never run them, where the cheapest covers are hiding — geo-targeting, dayparting, and the offers worth promoting.
  • Three fixes, in priority order: what we'd do first and what it should return. Yours to keep whether or not you ever hire us.

What Happens After You Hit Send

  • Within one business day: a real reply from the team with the audit findings — because we'll have already looked.
  • If it seems like a fit: a 30-minute working call. Bring your numbers if you're comfortable — covers, check averages, ad spend. We'll bring what we found.
  • Then a one-page plan: what we'd do, in what order, for how much. You can take it and run it yourself if you'd rather. Some people do, and they come back later. That's fine with us.

Good Fits, Honest Answers

We work with restaurants, bars and breweries, cafes, coffee shops, bakeries, and food trucks on the Sarasota Gulf Coast — from St. Armands Circle to Siesta Key to Anna Maria. If that's you, write to us. If you're outside food and beverage, we're genuinely not your agency, but email anyway and we'll try to point you somewhere good.

Prefer plain email? info@peakseasonmedia.com reaches the same inbox as the form. We don't publish a phone number — everything in writing means nothing gets lost between a lunch rush and a dinner service, and you'll never get a cold call from us.

Not sure what to ask for yet? Start with the pages that match your business — restaurants, bars & breweries, cafes, coffee shops, bakeries, or food trucks — then tell us which parts sounded like your Tuesday.

A Note on Timing

There's no wrong month to ask for the audit, but there are smarter ones. The best engagements on this coast start in late spring or summer — when you finally have time to think, and when the groundwork we lay (Google rankings, review velocity, ad audiences, email lists) has a full runway before the January wave arrives. If you're writing in February with a packed dining room, wonderful: let's capture that surge before it flies home. If you're writing in September staring at an empty patio, even better — that's exactly the moment a season-mapped plan earns its keep.

One more thing worth saying plainly: we only take on clients we believe we can move numbers for, and we cap how many businesses we work with in any one neighborhood or category. If your closest competitor got here first, we'll tell you straight and stay out of the conflict. It's a small coast; reputations are the only currency that survives the off-season.
