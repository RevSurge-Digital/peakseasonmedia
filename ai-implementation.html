AI Implementation

AI That Covers the Shifts Nobody Is Rostered For

Practical automations for small food-and-beverage teams — after-hours answering, review responses and enquiry routing built on your menu and your voice, with guardrails that hand off to a human instead of guessing.

Contact Us for a Free Marketing Audit

The Front of House You Cannot Staff

The work that gets dropped in a small food business is never the cooking. It is the message that came in at 9:40 p.m. asking whether you take reservations for eight, the review that sat unanswered for eleven days, the catering enquiry that went to an inbox nobody checks on a Sunday. Every one of those is revenue that walked, and none of them are worth a hire.

That is the gap this fills. Not “AI strategy” and not a chatbot bolted to your homepage that answers questions wrong in your voice. Specific, boring automations that cover the hours you cannot: answering the questions you get asked forty times a week, drafting review responses that sound like you, routing the enquiries that need a human to a human quickly.

We build these on your actual content — your menu, your hours, your policies, your voice — and we put guardrails on them. An assistant that does not know the answer says so and hands off. Confidently wrong is worse than silent when the question is about an allergy.

The AI Playbook

  • After-hours answeringhours, menu, parking, allergens, private events
  • Review responsesdrafted in your voice, approved by you
  • Enquiry routingcatering & large parties to a human, fast
  • Content draftingspecials, events & social, from your menu
  • Reportingplain-language monthly, not a dashboard login
  • Guardrailsescalates rather than guessing

It compounds with the rest: review velocity feeds local SEO & AEO, and faster enquiry response makes every dollar of restaurant advertising worth more.

An assistant that says “let me get a person” is worth ten that guess about an allergy.

Start with one thing

We do not roll out six automations at once. We pick the one leaking the most revenue — usually unanswered enquiries or stale reviews — run it for a month, measure it, and only then add the next. Small teams do not have the slack to absorb a bad rollout.

You keep the last word

Nothing goes out under your name that you have not approved a pattern for. Review responses are drafted for a one-tap approval, not auto-published. Your reputation is the asset; the automation just removes the typing.

What This Is Not

It is not a replacement for your staff, and any agency telling a twelve-person restaurant that AI will cut headcount is selling something. Your servers are the product. This covers the hours nobody is rostered for and the admin nobody wants: the 9:40 p.m. message, the eleven-day-old review, the catering form that arrived during a Saturday double.

It is also not a science project. Everything we build has a number attached before it ships — enquiries answered within an hour, review response rate, catering quotes sent per month. If an automation cannot be tied to one of those, we do not build it, and if it stops earning its keep we turn it off.

Good questions

Questions Operators Ask Us

Is this just a chatbot on our website?

No. A generic chatbot trained on the internet will confidently invent a gluten-free menu you do not have. What we build is grounded in your actual content — your menu, hours, policies and past answers — and it is wired into the places enquiries genuinely arrive: your website, your Google Business Profile messages, and your inbox. When it does not know, it says so and gets a person.

Will it sound like a robot wrote it?

Only if you let it. We build the voice from what you have already written — your existing review responses, your menu descriptions, the way you actually talk to guests — and you approve the patterns before anything goes out. Most owners find the drafts read more like them than the rushed replies they were typing at midnight.

Do we need to be technical to run this?

No. The whole point is that a small team should not need an operations manager to run software. Approvals happen on your phone in a couple of taps, reporting arrives monthly in plain language, and we handle everything underneath it. If a tool needs training to use, we have built the wrong tool.

What does it cost, and how do we know it works?

It is scoped like everything else we do — to your operation, as a fixed monthly line item, with the number it is meant to move agreed before we build. Usually that is enquiries answered within the hour, review response rate, or catering quotes sent per month. We start with the single automation leaking the most revenue rather than rolling out six at once, so the return shows up inside the first month or we change course.

What Is Falling Through the Gaps?

Tell us where enquiries go unanswered — the late-night messages, the reviews, the catering forms. The free audit comes back with the three we would automate first.
